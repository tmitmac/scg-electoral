How to Vote

Everything you need to know to participate in Square Country elections.

Voting Methods

In-Person Voting

Traditional voting at your assigned polling place on Election Day. Polls are open from 7:00 AM to 8:00 PM.

  • Most common method
  • Same-day registration available
  • Assistance available for voters with disabilities
More about in-person voting

Mail-In Voting

Vote from home by requesting an absentee ballot. Available to all registered voters without requiring an excuse.

  • Request deadline: 7 days before Election Day
  • Postmark deadline: Election Day
  • Track your ballot online
Request mail-in ballot

Early Voting

Vote in person before Election Day at designated early voting locations throughout Square Country.

  • Available 15 days before Election Day
  • Weekdays: 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM
  • Weekends: 9:00 AM - 4:00 PM
Find early voting locations

Accessible Voting

Special accommodations for voters with disabilities, including accessible voting machines and curbside voting.

  • Audio ballots available
  • Large print and braille options
  • Curbside voting at all polling places

Frequently Asked Questions

Acceptable forms of ID include a Square Country driver's license, state ID card, passport, military ID, or tribal identification card. Voters without ID may sign an affidavit of identity.

You can check your registration status online through our Voter Portal or by contacting your local election office. You'll need to provide your name, date of birth, and address to verify your registration.

If you've moved within Square Country, you can update your registration online up to 30 days before an election or at your new polling place on Election Day. If you've moved from another state, you'll need to register in Square Country.

To vote in Square Country, you must be a citizen, at least 18 years old by Election Day, a resident of Square Country, and not currently serving a felony sentence in prison.

If voting in person, notify a poll worker immediately. They can issue you a new ballot. For mail-in ballots, contact your local election office for instructions on obtaining a replacement ballot.
